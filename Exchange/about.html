Leaving from leeds through to Heathrow then on to Chicago then Madison no one had mentioned that along with the extreme cold in winter they have extreme heat and humidity as well. So there I was wearing 4 layers to take weight out of my suitcase....

So I begin with a cautionary tale that Wisconsin has much greater temperature and humidity extremes than the UK, in summer it reaches 30 degrees C and 70-80% humidity easily, and then in the depths of winter a dry wind coming off of lake Mendota can bring windchill to minus 40-50 degrees C.

However this awesome place, now you are prepared well, makes up for its climate with shiny stars to spare.

Madison as a city is the state capital of Wisconsin, home to about 300,000 people, annd home to the university of Wisconsin - Madison (the badgers, uw-madison and a host of other colloquialisms).

UW-Madison is the flagship college and the largest of the colleges in the University of Wisconsin system which has campuses all over the state. The UW-Madison is ranked consistently as one of the best universities in the world, having played host to 19 Nobel Laureates and a whole host of other impressive prize winners and acheivers.

Engineering here at UW-Madison is again world renowned, especially the Nuclear Engineering program with its own reactor and plasma experiments. Mechanical Engineering here, although not as revered is still held in high international regard with its engine research group being one of the biggest of its kind and one of the most cited.

The Engineering campus is amazing, its comprised of 7 buildings, with a library, a building almost completely dedicated to student engineering societies and the student workshop, and multiple department specific buildings. All the buildings center on engineering mall and are right in the middle of campus next to one of the student union buildings, Union South, and next to Camp Randall, the 80000 seat home of the Wisconsin Badgers American Football team.
