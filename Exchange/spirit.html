It's your first saturday morning in Mad town, early on, about 630, time for the farmer's market on capital square, you've been told you just have to go! It's early so maybe it won't be busy and you can stroll round at your leisure before going and gtting ready for the football game you have tickets to see in Camp Randall. Little did you realise that the very fact it's a home game would throw the idea of empty streets out the window.
You walk from your house and arrive near state street to be greeted by a flood of red, you walked on the bike path to get there and passed car after truck with grills being set up and tailgate parties erupting around the car parks.
Welcome to a Badger Gameday!!
Warwick manages to build a small amount of momentum for the varsity Ice Hockey game, but it is nothing compared to the average game day. It is a special kind of enthusiasm, that emerges in all its glory when its a ice hockey game, basketball, football and any other varsity sport. It's always there bubbling below the surface with a wisconsin t shirt or cap being common dress and every photo op has to be framed by throwing the 'dub' (W sign with your hands).
