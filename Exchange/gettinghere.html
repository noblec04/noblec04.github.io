The First step is acquiring ones Visa.
This is quite the process, and can be unnerving, but is easy enough as long as you don't put it off. The Exchange University will send you the documents you are required to take to the Embassy in London, you have to make the appointment.
first you will be called up to have your documents processed, to make sure everything is in order, then the 2nd time you are called up is the interview, this is used to make sure you are not planning on illegally immigrating.


Flying from Heathrow to Chicago, I was adviced was going to be the cheapest thing to do, but this would require organising getting from Chicago to Madison, there are perfectly good bus services between the cities however not being used to the area, and knowing I would be jetlagged I went for doing a connecting flight to Madison, Dane County airport.

Because I was arriving a day before I could move into accomodation, I was going to be in a bit of tight spot and ws almost thinking of exploring Madison for the evening before I could move in, however I found Madison Friends of International Students (MFIS). MFIS is a volunteer orgnisation, where local residents (Usually Alumni) volunteer to take in an international student to help them adjust for the 1st couple of dys and weeks, and they usually stay in contact with the students throughout their program.
I was amazingly lucky to be paired with a couple who were really generous, they helped me move in, and showed me round the city, and have then invited me to events throughout the year such as thanksgiving.
